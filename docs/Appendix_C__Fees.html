Appendix C – Withdrawal Fees and Merchant Fees

What Are Merchant Fees in RapidPay?

A merchant fee is a charge applied when users spend in the system. The purpose of merchant fees in RapidPay is to encourage users to spend within time window to avoid a decay in spending value while providing income for RapidPay.

What Are Withdrawal/Exit Fees in RapidPay?

A withdrawal or exit fee is a small charge applied when users take money out of the system — either after missing the spend window (post-decay) or without spending at all.

NOTE: 75+% must be spent in order to avoid decay

Purpose of Withdrawal/Exit Fees

  1. Prevents Late-Stage Deal Manipulation: Without a fee, users could top up, view all deals, cherry-pick none, and then withdraw without consequence.
  2. Discourages Passive Holding: Keeps users from treating RapidPay like a savings wallet. Instead, it nudges engagement.

User Behavior Fee Table

  • Spends 75%+ within 48 hours: ❌ No fee – Gets full value and rewards
  • Spends 75%+ within 48–96 hours: ✅ 5% Merchant fee – Decay after 2–4 days
  • Spends 75%+ within 96–144 hours: ✅ 8% Merchant fee – Decay after 4–6 days
  • Withdraws balance after 7 days unused: ✅ 10% fee
  • Withdraws within first 24h of top-up unused: ✅ 10% fee – Prevents “peek-and-leave” abuse
  • Withdrawn after receiving payment: ✅ 2% fee – General Withdrawal fee

1% of fees, except withdrawal fees, go to companies to offset late payment losses.
